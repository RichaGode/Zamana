Political Forum

America prides itself as a nation built of immigrants-- with the notion of "American diversity" comes the hope of political inclusion and participation. Join Club Zamana as we discuss issues of representation and identity in political, social, and cultural contexts. While exploring questions related to what it means to be a South Asian American, Club Zamana hopes to facilitate important and timely discussion about our multicultural society. Speakers include Indian-American politician, Raj Goyle, Rochelle Almeida, and Urban monk Gadadhara Pandit Dasa.

Club Zamana is proud to present its first Political Forum. The event, taking place on February 13, 2015, will feature a mix of talks, discussion forums, and other events on the topic of South Asians in America.

We are excited to announce the following speakers:

Raj Goyle - An Indian Democratic Politician talking about South Asian Relations to Politics in America

Rochelle Almeida - A Masters teacher of Liberal Studies in New York University speaking on The Changing Face of South Asians in America over the past 25 years

Gadadhara Pandit Dasa - An author, speaker, and urban monk

Our forum will also feature discussion lead by Sewa on South Asian Interfaith Issues in America

Lunch will be provided as well. We hope to see you there!
